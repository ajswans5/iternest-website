Founder Journal

The Blob Incident

July 2026

Today felt different.

Not because we built another screen.

Not because we fixed another bug.

Today felt different because IterNest finally became something I was proud to show someone else.

I showed my husband the prototype.

He smiled and said, “That looks really cool.”

That probably sounds small.

It was not small.

When you are building something from scratch, most of the work happens in a fog. You are looking at broken pieces, half-built screens, weird edge cases, placeholder text, and ideas that still feel too fragile to explain clearly.

You know what you are trying to make.

But no one else can quite see it yet.

Today, someone else saw it.

The Blob Incident

The moment I will probably remember from this week is what I am now calling the Blob Incident.

The weekly view was loading.

Technically, it worked.

Visually, it did not.

Everything showed up as one giant blob.

The tasks were there.

The structure was there.

The data was technically doing what it was supposed to do.

But the experience was wrong.

It looked chaotic.

It felt overwhelming.

It was exactly the opposite of what IterNest is supposed to create.

And that was the useful part.

Because the second I saw it, I knew what was wrong.

Not just with the layout.

With the feeling.

IterNest cannot simply collect information.

It has to make the week feel lighter.

It has to reduce the mental pileup, not recreate it in digital form.

So we fixed it.

What the App Is Starting to Become

The weekly view is now starting to feel like the center of the app.

It is where scattered tasks become something visible.

Where family rhythms start to take shape.

Where the plan does not have to be perfect in order to be useful.

That matters to me.

I do not want IterNest to become another productivity system that makes people feel like they are failing if they do not use it perfectly.

I want it to feel forgiving.

Useful.

Calm.

Something you can return to after a messy day without feeling behind.

That is a hard thing to build.

But this week, for the first time, I could see it.

Showing It to Someone Else

There is a vulnerability in showing someone a prototype.

You know everything that is missing.

You know the rough edges.

You know the features that are still only half-real.

You know what broke ten minutes earlier.

But you show it anyway.

Because at some point, building in isolation stops being helpful.

You need another pair of eyes.

You need to know whether the thing you have been carrying in your head is beginning to translate.

Today, it did.

Not fully.

Not perfectly.

But enough.

What Comes Next

Next, we keep tightening the experience.

The weekly view needs refinement.

The onboarding needs to feel clear.

The app needs to help families get from “everything is floating around in my head” to “I can see what matters this week.”

That is the work.

And after today, it feels more real.

There is still a long way to go.

But the shape is there now.

The blob is gone.

The week is visible.

We’re building it.

Calm, practical workflow tools for families and founders.
